🧳 What to Pack

  • Light-to-medium jacket (evenings get cool)
  • Comfortable walking shoes — cobblestones are real
  • One outfit for a nice dinner (not a suit, just sharp)
  • Compact folding umbrella
  • Layers — mornings are cool, afternoons warmer
  • Comfortable day bag or small backpack

💡 September Perks

  • Sainte-Chappelle: no hour-long summer queues
  • Versailles gardens still lush, way less crowded
  • Restaurant reservations easier to get
  • Café terraces still open — Parisians love autumn
  • Musée d'Orsay: manageable waits with advance tickets
  • Montmartre artists still out in full force

One of Paris's great "big monument" days. These are all close together — you can hit all of them. Book Sainte-Chappelle in advance.

Day Plan

  • Notre-Dame Cathedral — reopened December 2024 after the 2019 fire. The restoration is extraordinary. Book your free timed entry slot at notredame.paris in advance. Go for the history, the architecture, the feeling of standing inside 800 years of Paris.
  • Sainte-Chappelle — steps away from Notre-Dame. Jimmy says: "a masterclass in historical stained glass. Breathtaking." This is his #1 recommendation on the entire trip. 15 massive windows, 1,113 stained glass panels, built in the 1240s. Buy tickets in advance at sainte-chapelle.fr — the line without tickets can be 2+ hours.
  • Walk west along the river toward the Louvre. You don't have to go in — the grounds, the glass pyramid, and the Tuileries Garden are stunning on their own. If you want to see the inside (Mona Lisa etc), book well in advance; lines without tickets are enormous.
  • Walk up Champs-Élysées to the Arc de Triomphe. It's touristy but beautiful. The avenue is lined with plane trees going golden in September.
  • End the day in the Latin Quarter (5th & 6th arr) — wander the medieval streets, browse the bookshops, explore Place Maubert and Rue Mouffetard.

Restaurants

Lunch
Café de Flore or Les Deux Magots
172 Blvd Saint-Germain / 6 Place Saint-Germain · Two legendary Paris literary cafés. Croques, salads, café au lait. Go for the atmosphere as much as the food — Sartre, de Beauvoir, Hemingway all came here.
Dinner
Le Procope
13 Rue de l'Ancienne-Comédie, 6th · Oldest restaurant in Paris, founded 1686. Benjamin Franklin and Voltaire drank here. Classic French brasserie food in a historic setting. Walk-in friendly in September.
Alt Dinner
Brasserie Lipp
151 Blvd Saint-Germain · Classic Paris brasserie with 1900s décor, famous for choucroute and steak frites. A real Parisian institution. Go for the atmosphere.
💡 Sainte-Chappelle: the light through the windows is best on a partially sunny day (like this one). Go mid-morning. Notre-Dame: book your entry slot 2–3 days ahead at notredame.paris.

Dedicate this full day to Montmartre. Jimmy says it was his favorite and he wishes he'd spent more time. You won't run out of things to see.

Day Plan

  • Get here by cab, e-bike rental, or Métro (Abbesses stop, Line 12). From the Marais it's a bit of a hike — take a cab or Vélib' bike to save energy for walking Montmartre itself.
  • Sacré-Cœur Basilica — Jimmy says: "one of the most awe-inspiring, religious feeling places I've ever been." Climb the stairs for the view of all Paris spread below you. The basilica interior is free. The steps and terrace in September morning light are extraordinary.
  • Explore the winding streets of Montmartre village — Place du Tertre (local artists selling paintings), Rue Lepic, Rue des Abbesses. Stop at a café wherever you feel like it.
  • Browse the artist stalls — people actually sell good work here, not just tourist prints. September means the artists are out in full force before winter.
  • Find the Clos Montmartre vineyard — a real, working vineyard inside Paris. The vines go golden in September. Take photos.
  • Evening: Moulin Rouge is right here. Book a show in advance — it's touristy but genuinely spectacular. Dinner + show packages available, or drinks before at a nearby bar.

River day. Let the Batobus do the navigating. The Jardin des Plantes alone is worth an entire afternoon.

Day Plan

  • Buy a Batobus day pass (batobus.com) — it's a hop-on/hop-off passenger boat on the Seine with stops at all major attractions. Excellent way to see Paris from the water and cover ground without taxis.
  • Take the Batobus to the Eiffel Tower stop (Champ de Mars). Jimmy says the grounds can be boring without going up — consider buying tickets for the top (book online at toureiffel.paris; September lines are much better than summer). The view from the top is genuine.
  • When done at the tower, get back on the Batobus and ride to the Jardin des Plantes stop.
  • Jardin des Plantes (King's Garden / Garden of Plants) — Jimmy spent an entire afternoon here and wanted more time. It's a 28-hectare botanical garden where medical herbs and scientific discoveries were catalogued since the 1600s. Walk every path. The greenhouse is exceptional. September means the gardens are still lush and flowering.
  • The Muséum National d'Histoire Naturelle is inside the garden — the Grande Galerie de l'Évolution is one of the best natural history museums in the world if you want to duck inside.

Restaurants

Lunch
Les Ombres
27 Quai Branly, 7th · Rooftop restaurant directly above the Musée du quai Branly with a glass roof and direct views of the Eiffel Tower. Reserve a window table. The food matches the view.
Dinner
Le Vent d'Armor
One of the highest rated restaurants by the Seine. 5-star dining experience. Very $$$, dress up, must reserve well in advance. Jimmy specifically recommends this for a special evening by the water. Worth every euro for a 10th anniversary dinner.
Alt Dinner
Café Constant
139 Rue Saint-Dominique, 7th · Chef Christian Constant's beloved neighborhood bistro. Excellent classic French food, no pretension, great wine. More accessible than the big splurge restaurants but the food is superb.
💡 Book Eiffel Tower summit tickets (toureiffel.paris) at least 1–2 weeks ahead. September is far less brutal than July–August but tickets still sell out. Le Vent d'Armor: call ahead for reservations well before your trip.

Day Plan

  • Musée d'Orsay — Jimmy's museum recommendation over the Louvre. "Lots of stuff from Renoir, Van Gogh, Monet. More accessible, wait times less, more space." This is the greatest Impressionist collection in the world. Buy tickets in advance at musee-orsay.fr — timed entry. Spend 2–3 hours minimum.

  • Walk to Église Saint-Sulpice — built beginning in 1646, the 3rd largest church in Paris. Jimmy says: "really pretty building." There's often an open-air market outside with vendors selling knick-knacks and local art during the day.
  • Browse the Saint-Sulpice neighborhood — one of the best shopping streets in Paris is just steps away.

If you're staying in the Marais, today is your neighborhood day. If on the Île, a 10-min walk gets you here.

Day Plan

  • Place des Vosges — Paris's oldest planned square, built 1612. Perfect symmetry, arcaded walkways, beautiful in autumn light. Victor Hugo lived on the corner (his house is now a museum). Sit in the garden in the center with a coffee.
  • Walk through the Jewish Quarter (Rue des Rosiers) — one of the most historically significant and charming streets in Paris. Excellent falafel (L'As du Fallafel at 34 Rue des Rosiers is legendary).
  • The Marais has the highest concentration of contemporary art galleries in Paris — many are free to enter and open without reservations. Browse as many as you want.
  • Centre Pompidou (if interested in modern art) — the building itself is a landmark. Book tickets in advance. The Brancusi studio outside is free and excellent.
  • Evening: the Marais has the best bar scene in Paris — the Little Red Door cocktail bar is in the Marais (Rue Charlot). Perfect for tonight.

Cooler, crisper day — perfect for the cemetery and the catacombs. The Canal Saint-Martin in the evening is lovely in the autumn light.

Day Plan

  • Père Lachaise Cemetery (20th arr) — walkable from the Marais. Opened in the early 1800s. 44 hectares of winding cobblestone paths, elaborate 19th-century monuments, enormous old trees. Frédéric Chopin, Oscar Wilde, Molière, Édith Piaf, Jim Morrison, Victor Noir all buried here. Not morbid — genuinely beautiful, especially in autumn. Go 4–6pm for the best light.
  • The Catacombs (14th arr, south Paris) — 6 million Parisians buried in the underground tunnels, organized into a macabre ossuary by Héricart de Thury in the early 19th century. Book tickets online well in advance at catacombes.paris.fr — walk-in queue can be 2+ hours. A genuinely unforgettable hour underground.
  • Note: Père Lachaise and the Catacombs are in opposite parts of the city — consider doing them on different days if you want to linger at each, or do them in sequence via Métro.
  • Evening: Canal Saint-Martin (10th arr) — walk the iron footbridges and tree-lined towpaths at dusk. The canal neighborhood has Paris's best casual restaurant scene. Perfect for a slow dinner.

Restaurants

Lunch

Date Nights
in Paris

100 ideas for 10 days · mix and match · you won't run out

You have 10 days and 10 evenings. These are organized by mood — pick whatever fits the night. Some are free, some are splurges, all of them are good.

🎨 Active & Creative
Painting Class at Pauline Fraisse Atelier▼
Book a 2–3 hour oil or watercolor workshop at Pauline Fraisse Art & Culture — small groups, taught in English and French, classic Parisian atelier setting. You'll each paint the same subject and end up with completely different results. Walk to dinner afterward with your paintings wrapped under your arm.
📍 paulinefraisse.com · English-language · book ahead · ~€80pp
Watercolor Walk Along the Seine▼
Buy a small watercolor travel kit from Gilbert Joseph (art supply store on Boulevard Saint-Michel), grab a sketchbook, and pick a spot along the Seine quais — Pont Saint-Louis, looking back at Notre-Dame, is a classic. Spend an hour painting whatever you see. No skill required. The point is the afternoon light and the effort of actually looking at something together.
📍 Gilbert Joseph · 26 Blvd Saint-Michel · supplies ~€20 total · free activity
Pottery Class in the Marais▼
L'Atelier de la Terre in the Marais offers drop-in and booked pottery sessions — wheel throwing and hand building. There is something inherently fun about doing pottery together. Sessions are 2 hours. They fire and glaze your piece and can ship it home if you want a keepsake from the anniversary trip. Book at least a week ahead.
📍 atelierdelaterre.com · Marais · ~€60–80pp · ships internationally
Perfume Workshop at Fragonard▼
Fragonard's museum and workshop on Boulevard des Capucines runs 2-hour perfume creation workshops where you blend your own scent guided by a professional parfumeur. You smell through 20+ raw ingredients and build something personal. At the end you each leave with a custom 100ml bottle of your own perfume. A genuinely romantic anniversary activity.
📍 Fragonard · 9 Rue Scribe, 9th · €60–90pp · book online · 2 hours
Macaron Making at Cook & Go▼
Cook & Go in the Marais does fun 1.5-hour baking workshops — macarons, éclairs, madeleines. You pipe, fill, and assemble your own, then eat them while still warm. Walk out with a box of your own Parisian macarons. The instructor keeps it relaxed and genuinely fun, not a cooking school.
📍 cookandgo.fr · 4 Rue de Bretagne, 3rd · ~€60pp · 1.5 hours
Wine Blending Workshop at O Chateau▼
O Chateau offers wine blending masterclasses — you sample base varietals and blend your own cuvée, then design a label for it. Takes about 2 hours. You each blend a different bottle, taste each other's, argue about it. A great anniversary keepsake.
📍 O Chateau · 68 Rue Jean-Jacques Rousseau, 1st · ~€80pp · book ahead
Bread Baking Class at La Cuisine Paris▼
La Cuisine Paris runs a French bread and viennoiserie class — baguettes, fougasse, croissants. 3-hour class taught in English, small groups. You eat what you make. Walk out carrying a paper bag of your own baguettes through Paris and tell me that doesn't feel perfect.
📍 lacuisineparis.com · 80 Quai de l'Hôtel de Ville, 4th · ~€110pp · 3 hours
Cocktail-Making Masterclass▼
Several Paris cocktail schools run couple-friendly workshops — 2 hours, you make 3–4 classic cocktails, learn technique, and drink what you build. It's casual and fun. Then go out and order drinks confidently for the rest of the trip. Search Airbnb Experiences for "Paris cocktail class."
📍 Airbnb Experiences · 1st–3rd arr · ~€70–90pp · 2 hours
Salsa & Latin Dance Class in Pigalle▼
SoPi (South Pigalle) has Latin dance studios that run drop-in beginner classes on weeknights — salsa, bachata, kizomba. Studio Univers Salsa (9th arr) is one of the best for beginners. Classes run 1.5 hours, then the floor opens for social dancing. Even if you're terrible (especially if you're terrible), it's a guaranteed good time.
📍 South Pigalle · 9th arr · ~€15pp drop-in · weeknight classes from 8pm
Photography Walk — Give Each Other a Theme▼
Each of you picks a secret theme ("doors," "reflections," "people reading," "anything red") and spend two hours shooting Paris on your phones without telling the other what your theme is. Meet back at a café, order wine, and show each other your shots. Guess the theme. It's a genuinely fun game that makes you look at the city differently.
📍 Any neighborhood · free · just your phone · best in Marais or Saint-Germain
Bike Ride Along the Canal Saint-Martin▼
Rent bikes from Vélib' (Paris's bike share — use the app) and ride up the Canal Saint-Martin to Parc de la Villette and back. The canal is one of Paris's loveliest areas — iron footbridges, tree-lined towpaths, cool neighborhood cafés. Stop when you feel like it. Best on a Sunday morning when the canal towpaths are closed to cars.
📍 Canal Saint-Martin · 10th–19th arr · Vélib' bikes ~€5/day · 8–12km loop
Flower Arranging Workshop▼
Several Marais florists run 2-hour floral arranging workshops — you pick your stems, learn wire and structure, and build your own arrangement. Combine it with the Île de la Cité flower market (steps from your hotel) in the morning. You each build a bouquet. She keeps hers. Or he does. You decide.
📍 Île de la Cité flower market + Marais workshops · ~€50–70pp
Life Drawing Class in a Montmartre Studio▼
Montmartre has been an artist's neighborhood since the 1880s and several studios still run life drawing evenings — open sessions with a model, charcoal and paper provided. No skill required. Check Meetup.com (search "Paris life drawing") for drop-in sessions. Good red wine from a nearby cave, a Tuesday evening, a pad of newsprint. Very Paris.
📍 Montmartre · 18th arr · Meetup.com for sessions · ~€15pp drop-in
Morning Yoga in the Tuileries▼
Free yoga and fitness classes sometimes run in the Tuileries Garden in late summer/early fall. Check the Paris Parks schedule. Otherwise, Café Kitsune (in the garden) is the best coffee in that part of Paris. Perfect slow morning before a big day — Louvre pyramid on one side, Eiffel Tower in the distance.
📍 Jardin des Tuileries · 1st arr · free · check paris.fr for schedule
Rent a Rowboat in Bois de Vincennes▼
Take the Métro Line 1 to Château de Vincennes and rent a rowboat on Lac Daumesnil — Paris's largest lake, inside a massive forested park. Row out to the island in the middle, tie up, sit on the grass. Bring a picnic: natural wine, bread, cheese, fruit. You'll feel like you left the city entirely. Rowboats are €14/hour.
📍 Lac Daumesnil, Bois de Vincennes · Métro Line 1 · €14/hr · open daily

Candelaria — Hidden Speakeasy Behind a Taqueria▼

Rooftop Cocktails at Perruche (Printemps)▼

Natural Wine Bar Crawl▼
Build a 3-bar evening: start at Aux Deux Amis (45 Rue Oberkampf) — legendary small plates and natural wine — then to Septime La Cave (3 Rue Basfroi, 11th) for a bottle from their outstanding list, then finish at Racines 2 (39 Rue de l'Arbre Sec, 1st). Champagne Bar at Hôtel Particulier Montmartre▼
Hidden inside an 18th-century private mansion in Montmartre — you enter through a gate on Avenue Junot, walk through a garden, and arrive at an intimate bar serving excellent Champagne and small plates. The setting is extraordinary: a candlelit mansion garden in Montmartre in September. This is the rare Paris place that lives up to its setting.
📍 23 Ave Junot, 18th · bar open evenings · Champagne ~€20/glass
Le Baron Rouge — Wine by the Barrel▼
1 Rue Théophile Roussel, 12th, next to Marché d'Aligre. Bring your own container and fill it from the barrels, or drink standing at the bar. Cash only. Sunday mornings after the market are legendary. Berthillon Ice Cream Walk on the Island▼
31 Rue Saint-Louis en l'Île — Paris's most famous ice cream. Get two scoops of whatever looks best, walk the island's main street, find the steps on the Quai d'Anjou facing the river and sit. That's it. September evenings are cool but not cold. Sometimes the simplest thing is the best thing.

Absinthe Tasting at a Belle Époque Bar▼
La Fée Verte (8 Rue de la Roquette, 11th) is the Paris temple of absinthe — named after the Green Fairy. They carry 50+ absinthes, serve it properly (with the drip, the sugar cube, the spoon), and the atmosphere is pure 1890s Belle Époque. The art nouveau room and the ritual of the pour is the event.
📍 8 Rue de la Roquette, 11th · no res · open from 6pm · ~€10–15/glass
Cheese & Wine Tasting at Androuet▼
Androuet is one of Paris's finest fromageries and runs guided cheese & wine tasting experiences — 1.5 hours, 5–6 cheeses from different regions, paired wines, explained by a maître fromager. Educational but not stuffy. Book online. You'll never buy supermarket cheese again.
📍 Multiple locations · main: 37 Rue de Verneuil, 7th · ~€55pp · androuet.com
Dinner at Septime — Paris's Best Restaurant▼
80 Rue de Charonne, 11th. Consistently one of the top 10 restaurants in the world. Market-driven, seasonal, inventive French cuisine. Reservations open 30 days in advance at exactly noon — set an alarm and book the moment you can. Worth every effort for a special occasion anniversary dinner.
📍 80 Rue de Charonne, 11th · book 30 days out at noon · ~€90pp
Wine & Jazz at Autour d'un Verre▼
116 Rue de Richelieu, 2nd — a relaxed wine bar that doubles as a jazz venue on weekend nights. Natural wine list, small food plates, live jazz from 9pm Friday and Saturday. Not a performance venue — more like a bar where musicians happen to be playing. Great wine, real jazz, low pretension.
📍 116 Rue de Richelieu, 2nd · jazz Fri & Sat from 9pm · ~€40–60pp
Café de Flore with Books on a Weekday▼
172 Boulevard Saint-Germain. One of Paris's great literary cafés. Go Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon. Get a corner table on the terrace, order a café crème and a citron pressé, bring a book each, and sit for two hours. Simone de Beauvoir wrote here. Sartre wrote here. The city keeps moving. 📍 172 Blvd Saint-Germain, 6th · terrace · ~€15pp for drinks · weekday afternoon
Build a Market Picnic▼
Hit three spots the same morning: Marché d'Aligre (12th) for vegetables and fruit; a good fromagerie for one perfect cheese; a cave à vins for a bottle of natural white or rosé. Assemble at the Quai de Béthune on the south side of Île Saint-Louis, facing the river. Spread a blanket, open the wine. The whole exercise costs under €40 and is the best lunch in Paris.

Dinner at Lapérouse — Private Salon▼
51 Quai des Grands-Augustins, 6th. Request one of their private salons — candlelit, intimate, historic, looking out over the Seine. 17th-century building. They specialize in romantic special occasions. For an anniversary dinner, it's worth the call to reserve a private room specifically.

Late Night at a Classic Paris Brasserie▼
La Coupole (102 Blvd du Montparnasse) is open until 1am and has served Paris since 1927. Giant art-deco room, outstanding seafood platters, excellent champagne. Go at 10:30pm after a show or a museum night. Order the grand plateau de fruits de mer and a bottle of Sancerre. This is Paris doing Paris.

Champagne Tasting — Learn the Difference▼
O Chateau runs Champagne-focused tastings — Blanc de Blancs vs. Blanc de Noirs, grower Champagnes vs. grandes maisons. About 2 hours, 5–6 pours, one expert. 👟 Walking & Wandering
Golden Hour Walk Around Notre-Dame▼
With Notre-Dame's exterior restoration complete by 2026, this is the right trip to see it properly. Go at 7:30–8pm when the light is golden. Walk the full circumference — the apse from Pont de l'Archevêché, the south portal, the rose window, the north façade. Then cross to the island and walk back along Quai d'Anjou. The rebuilt spire at golden hour is going to be something.
📍 Île de la Cité · free · best 7:30–8:30pm in September · 45-minute walk
The Passages Couverts — 19th-Century Glass Arcades▼
Paris has 20+ covered passages from the 1820s–1860s — glass-roofed iron arcades that predate department stores. The finest: Galerie Vivienne (2nd), Passage des Panoramas (2nd), and Galerie Véro-Dodat (1st). Spend an afternoon drifting through antique print shops, old toy stores, stamp dealers, and cafés frozen in 1875.
📍 2nd arr mainly · free entry · open daily until 7–8pm · 2-hour wander
Promenade Plantée — Paris's Original High Line▼
Paris's elevated rail trail (built 30 years before New York's High Line) — 4.7km along a disused 19th-century viaduct from Bastille east. The arches below house art galleries and craft workshops. The elevated path is planted with roses and lavender. In September still in bloom and essentially empty by 6pm.
📍 Bastille, 12th arr · free · 4.7km elevated · best in morning or late afternoon
Montmartre's Hidden Stairways & Vineyard▼
From Abbesses Métro, take Rue Lepic, then cut east through the Clos Montmartre vineyard path, past Salvador Dalí's house, through the hidden garden of la Bonne Franquette, and up the back stairs to the top. You'll see nobody. In September the vines are turning gold. Then catch the city view at 8pm. Walk back through the charming streets of Abbesses.
📍 18th arr · start at Abbesses Métro · 2-hour wander · free · evening is best
Walk Every Bridge — Make a Wish Game▼
There are 37 bridges across the Seine through Paris. The inner stretch from Pont d'Iéna to Pont de Bercy has about 20 — walkable in 3 hours. The game: at each bridge you each make a wish. The rule: different wish at each bridge. Start at Pont d'Iéna under the Eiffel Tower, walk east. Stop for wine when you feel like it. Perfect for an anniversary evening.
📍 Pont d'Iéna east · 3–4 hours · free · best started at 5:30pm
Belleville Street Art Walk▼
Belleville (20th arr) is Paris's most concentrated street art neighborhood — murals on every surface, commissioned works from international artists. Start at Couronnes Métro, walk north up Boulevard de Belleville, turning into side streets. Follow with dinner at one of the Vietnamese or Chinese restaurants on the boulevard — Belleville is also Paris's best Asian food neighborhood.
📍 20th arr · Couronnes Métro · 2-hour walk · free · afternoon or evening
Latin Quarter's Medieval Streets at Night▼
The 5th and 6th arrondissements at night are completely different from daytime. Start at Place Maubert, walk through Rue Mouffetard (oldest market street in Paris, still cobbled), around the Panthéon, through the back streets to Boulevard Saint-Germain. Stop at a terrace for wine. These streets are basically unchanged since the Middle Ages. Best done 9–11pm.
📍 5th–6th arr · Place Maubert Métro · 2-hour walk · best 9–11pm
Canal Saint-Martin at Dusk▼
Walk from République north toward Jaurès as the sun goes down (~7pm in September). Iron footbridges, plane trees, locks, atmospheric evening light. Stop at any of the restaurants and bars along the canal — Chez Prune, or one of the outdoor terrace restaurants. The whole area feels like a village inside the city.
📍 10th arr · République Métro · 2km canal walk · best 7–9pm
Père Lachaise at Golden Hour▼
Go at 5–6pm on a weekday: quiet, golden light filtering through the enormous old trees, cats sleeping on tombstones. Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Édith Piaf, Marcel Proust, Frédéric Chopin. Hauntingly beautiful and not morbid at all. Oscar Wilde's tomb has been re-glassed after years of lipstick kisses. Worth an hour just wandering.
📍 20th arr · Père Lachaise Métro · free · closes at 6pm · go 4–6pm
Butte-aux-Cailles — The Hidden Village▼
One of Paris's best-kept secrets — a small village neighborhood of cobbled lanes, street art, outdoor pools (one has been here since 1924), and great bistros. Métro to Corvisart (Line 6), walk up. The streets haven't changed much since the 1920s. Excellent dinner options, zero tourists. A perfect evening for an anniversary when you want Paris to yourself.
📍 13th arr · Corvisart Métro · 1.5-hour wander · free · best evenings

Moulin Rouge — The Full Experience▼
82 Blvd de Clichy, 18th. The original, since 1889. Book the dinner + Champagne + show package — it's more expensive but the full event. The show is spectacular regardless of what you've heard. Dress up. September performances are less crowded than summer. Book at moulinrouge.fr. This is one of the iconic Paris experiences — do it at least once.

Opera at Palais Garnier▼
The most opulent opera house in the world — built 1861–1875 for Napoleon III, with a Marc Chagall ceiling painted in 1964. Even if opera isn't your thing, go for the building. Jazz at New Morning▼
7–9 Rue des Petites-Écuries, 10th — Paris's premier jazz club since 1981. Prince has played here. Keith Jarrett has played here. The September–October season is the best time to go — European jazz tours converge on Paris in autumn. Check newmorning.com for the calendar. Walk-in if not sold out; otherwise book ahead online.

Rooftop Cinema Under the Stars▼
Several Paris venues run outdoor cinema in late summer/early fall — MK2 Quai de Loire, La Cinémathèque, and pop-ups on rooftops. Films are typically in VO (original language with French subtitles). 📍 Various venues · cinemaenpleinair.com · ~€8–12pp · check September program
Louvre After Dark — Friday Night▼
The Louvre is open until 9:45pm on Friday nights — and the crowds are dramatically thinner after 6pm. If you're going to the Louvre at all, this is how to do it. The Egyptian antiquities wing, the Winged Victory of Samothrace, the Venus de Milo — all far more accessible on a Friday evening than a Tuesday afternoon. Buy timed tickets in advance.

Versailles Musical Fountains Show▼
The Grandes Eaux Musicales at Versailles runs on Saturdays and Sundays through September — all the garden fountains are turned on simultaneously with classical music playing through the grounds. An entirely different experience than a regular visit. The evening Grandes Eaux Nocturnes (musical fountains with fireworks) runs several times in September — check chateauversailles-spectacles.fr.

Boat Cruise at Sunset▼
The Bateaux Mouches dinner cruise departs from Pont de l'Alma and runs 2 hours on the Seine with dinner service. In September the sunset around 7pm lines up perfectly with the early dinner departure. It's touristy — but for an anniversary, a candlelit dinner on the Seine under the bridges of Paris is exactly the kind of thing you do once and remember forever. Book at bateaux-mouches.fr.

Cabaret at Crazy Horse▼
12 Avenue George V, 8th. Not the Moulin Rouge — Crazy Horse is Paris's most sophisticated cabaret, known for extraordinary lighting design and dance. Sainte-Chappelle Evening Concert▼
Classical music concerts are regularly held inside Sainte-Chappelle — imagine listening to Vivaldi or Handel inside those 15 stained-glass windows at night, candlelit. Picnic at the Eiffel Tower After Dark▼
The Champ de Mars is free to sit on. Every evening at dark, the Eiffel Tower sparkles with its light show (5 minutes every hour, on the hour, until 1am). Bring wine, cheese, bread, blankets. 💍 Anniversary Moments
Champagne at the Top of the Eiffel Tower▼
The Eiffel Tower's summit level (276m) has a Champagne bar — a glass of Moët at the very top of the tower, looking out over all of Paris. Buy summit tickets at toureiffel.paris in advance. September: clear autumn air, no summer haze. The Champagne glass at the top costs about €15. The view is priceless. This is not something you have to do — but for a 10th anniversary it's hard to top.

Pick a morning mid-trip. Find a café that suits you — terrace, good coffee, not rushed. Buy two postcards at the tabac next door. 🖼️ Museums & Galleries Worth Knowing
Musée Rodin — Most Beautiful Museum Garden in Paris▼
77 Rue de Varenne, 7th. The Thinker is in the garden. The Kiss is inside. The entire sculpture garden of the Hôtel Biron is one of the most beautiful spaces in Paris — rose bushes, gravel paths, Rodin bronzes everywhere. Admission €13, or just pay €4 for garden-only access. Go in the afternoon for the light. September: gardens at their last bloom of the year.
📍 77 Rue de Varenne, 7th · musee-rodin.fr · €13 full / €4 garden only · closed Mon
Musée de l'Orangerie — Monet's Water Lilies Room▼
Place de la Concorde, Tuileries Garden. Monet designed two oval rooms specifically for these paintings — the largest Water Lilies canvases he ever made, floor-to-ceiling, surrounding you completely. It's one of the most immersive art experiences in the world. The light in the oval rooms (natural light from above) changes through the day. Go at 2pm. Tickets €12.50, book ahead.
📍 Jardin des Tuileries, 1st arr · musee-orangerie.fr · €12.50 · closed Tue
Musée Picasso — The Marais▼
5 Rue de Thorigny, 3rd. Right in the heart of the Marais. The collection is drawn from Picasso's own personal holdings — paintings, drawings, ceramics, sculptures he kept for himself. A very different selection than you see in most Picasso retrospectives. Beautiful 17th-century Hôtel Salé building. Rarely crowded. Buy tickets online, go on a weekday.
📍 5 Rue de Thorigny, 3rd · musee-picasso.fr · €14 · closed Mon
Centre Pompidou — Modern Art + City Views▼
The building is as interesting as the collection — all the pipes and structure on the outside, revolutionary when built in 1977. The top-floor terrace has excellent views. The permanent collection is outstanding modern art. The Brancusi studio reconstruction in the plaza is free. If you're going to see any modern art in Paris, Pompidou over everything else.
📍 Place Georges-Pompidou, 4th · centrepompidou.fr · €14 · closed Tue
Fondation Louis Vuitton — Frank Gehry Building▼
Bois de Boulogne, 16th arr. Frank Gehry's spectacular glass building houses one of the world's best private contemporary art collections. The Fondation rotates major exhibitions — check fondationlouisvuitton.fr for the September show. Take Métro Line 1 to Les Sablons and then the free Fondation shuttle. The building exterior alone is extraordinary.
📍 Bois de Boulogne, 16th · fondationlouisvuitton.fr · €16 · check exhibitions
Musée de Cluny — Medieval Paris Underground▼
28 Rue Du Sommerard, 5th. Built on top of 1st-century Roman baths. Houses the Lady and the Unicorn tapestries (6 panels, 15th century, one of the greatest textile works ever made). The Roman thermal baths underneath are astonishing for being right in the middle of Paris. Rarely crowded. €9. If you have any interest in medieval art or Roman history, don't miss this.
📍 28 Rue Du Sommerard, 5th · musee-moyenage.fr · €9 · closed Tue

Practical Tips from Jimmy

Things worth knowing before you land.

🚇 Getting Around

Buy a carnet (book of 10 Métro tickets) or use the Navigo Découverte weekly pass (~€30, valid Mon–Sun) — it covers all Métro, RER, and bus in zones 1–5 including Versailles. The Paris Métro is excellent and runs until 1am (2am on weekends). Ubers are also widely available. Avoid taxis from the airport — pre-book a sedan or take the RER B train.

💶 Money & Tipping

Paris is mostly card-friendly, but carry €50–100 cash for smaller cafés, markets, and places like Le Baron Rouge. Tipping in France is optional and not expected — the service charge is included in the bill. Rounding up or leaving €1–2 on the table for a good meal is appreciated but not required. Don't over-tip — it looks American.

🗣️ Language

Always begin with "Bonjour" or "Bonsoir" — it's considered rude to launch straight into English. A simple "Bonjour, parlez-vous anglais?" (Do you speak English?) goes a long way. Most Paris restaurant and hotel staff speak English. The Parisians are not rude — they just expect basic courtesy, same as anyone. Lead with the greeting and you'll be fine.
