Day Plan

  • Musée des Arts et Métiers (60 rue Réaumur, 3rd) — proto-steampunk museum of science, industry and invention, built inside a 12th-century priory. Day Plan

Day Plan

  • Drive north (~1.5 hrs from Beaune). Vézelay — a hilltop UNESCO pilgrimage village with a Romanesque basilica that will stop you cold. The light inside at noon is extraordinary. Walk every lane — the village is tiny and perfect.

  • Stop at Époisses village — buy the famous washed-rind cheese directly from the fromagerie (Fromagerie Berthaut). Wrap it well. It's the best cheese in France and it smells like it.

Painting Class at Pauline Fraisse Atelier▼
Book a 2–3 hour oil or watercolor workshop at Pauline Fraisse Art & Culture (paulinefraisse.com) — small groups of 3–6, taught in English and French, classic Parisian atelier setting. You'll each paint the same subject and end up with completely different results, which is half the fun. She focuses on light and impressionist technique, which feels exactly right for Paris. Walk to dinner afterward with your paintings wrapped under your arm.
📍 paulinefraisse.com · English-language · book ahead · ~€80pp
Watercolor Walk Along the Seine▼
Buy a small watercolor travel kit from Gilbert Joseph (art supply store on Boulevard Saint-Michel), grab a sketchbook, and pick a spot along the Seine quais — Pont Saint-Louis, looking back at Notre-Dame, is a classic. Spend an hour painting whatever you see. No skill required. The point is the afternoon light, the company, and the effort of actually looking at something together.
📍 Gilbert Joseph · 26 Blvd Saint-Michel · supplies ~€20 total · free activity
Pottery Class in the Marais▼
L'Atelier de la Terre (atelierdelaterre.com) in the Marais offers drop-in and booked pottery sessions — wheel throwing and hand building. There is something inherently fun and a little ridiculous about doing pottery together, and you'll both walk out covered in clay and laughing. Sessions are 2 hours. They fire and glaze your piece and can ship it home if you want a keepsake. Book at least a week ahead.
📍 atelierdelaterre.com · Marais · ~€60–80pp · ships internationally
Perfume Workshop at Fragonard▼
Fragonard's museum and workshop on Boulevard des Capucines runs 2-hour perfume creation workshops where you blend your own scent guided by a "nose" (parfumeur). You smell through 20+ raw ingredients and build something personal. At the end you each leave with a custom 100ml bottle of your own perfume. It's far more intimate than it sounds — you're essentially answering the question "what do I want to smell like?" together.
📍 Fragonard · 9 Rue Scribe, 9th · €60–90pp · book online · 2 hours
Macaron Making at Cook & Go▼
Cook & Go in the Marais (cookandgo.fr) does fun 1.5-hour baking workshops — macarons, éclairs, madeleines. The macaron class can be adapted to vegan (aquafaba meringue) if you ask ahead. You pipe, fill, and assemble your own, then eat them while still warm. Walk out with a box of your own Parisian macarons. The instructor keeps it relaxed and genuinely fun, not a cooking school.
📍 cookandgo.fr · 4 Rue de Bretagne, 3rd · ~€60pp · 1.5 hours
Wine Blending Workshop at a Cave à Vins▼
O Chateau (o-chateau.com) and Château & Chais en Bourgogne both offer wine blending masterclasses — you sample base varietals from different plots and blend your own cuvée, then design a label for it. As someone who'll just have come back from Burgundy, this is a fun capstone. Takes about 2 hours. You each blend a different bottle, taste each other's, argue about it.
📍 O Chateau · 68 Rue Jean-Jacques Rousseau, 1st · ~€80pp · book ahead
Bread Baking Class at La Cuisine Paris▼
La Cuisine Paris (lacuisineparis.com) runs a French bread and viennoiserie class that's entirely plant-based friendly — baguettes, fougasse, croissants. It's a 3-hour class taught in English with professional instruction and small groups. You eat what you make. The smell alone is worth it. Walk out carrying a paper bag of your own baguettes through Paris and tell me that doesn't feel perfect.

Take the Métro Line 1 to Château de Vincennes and rent a rowboat on Lac Daumesnil — Paris's largest lake, inside a massive forested park with a medieval château on the edge. Get a corner table on the terrace, order a café crème and a citron pressé, bring a book each, and just sit for two hours. Simone de Beauvoir wrote here. Jean-Paul Sartre wrote here. 📍 172 Blvd Saint-Germain, 6th · terrace · ~€15pp for drinks · go weekday afternoon
Build a Market Picnic — Best Ingredients in Paris▼
Hit three spots on the same morning: the Marché d'Aligre (12th) for vegetables and fruit, Androuet fromagerie for one perfect cheese (Megi skips or tastes at her call), and a good cave à vins for a bottle of natural white or rosé. Assemble at the Quai de Béthune on the south side of Île Saint-Louis, facing the river. Spread a blanket, open the wine. The whole exercise costs under €40 and is the best lunch in Paris.
📍 Marché d'Aligre + Île Saint-Louis · Tuesday–Sunday mornings · ~€35–50 total
👟 Walking & Wandering
Golden Hour Walk Around Notre-Dame▼
With Notre-Dame's exterior restoration complete by 2026, this is the right trip to see it properly. Go at 8:30–9pm when the light is long and golden. Walk the full circumference of the cathedral — the apse from Pont de l'Archevêché, the south portal, the rose window, the north façade. Then cross to the island and walk back along Quai d'Anjou. The rebuilt spire at golden hour is going to be something.
📍 Île de la Cité · free · best 8:30–9:30pm in June · 45-minute walk
The Passages Couverts — 19th-Century Glass Arcades▼
Paris has 20+ covered passages from the 1820s–1860s — glass-roofed iron arcades that predate department stores. The finest are Galerie Vivienne (2nd), Passage des Panoramas (2nd), and Galerie Véro-Dodat (1st). Spend an afternoon drifting through them: antique print shops, coin dealers, old-fashioned toy stores, stamp collectors, and the best stamp-and-coin dealers in France. Each passage has its own character. They close at 7–8pm so go afternoon.
📍 2nd arr mainly · free entry · open daily until 7–8pm · 2-hour wander
Promenade Plantée — Elevated Rail Trail▼
Paris's original High Line (built 30 years before New York's) — a 4.7km elevated walkway along a disused 19th-century rail viaduct from Bastille to Bois de Vincennes. The arches below are art galleries and craft workshops. The elevated path is planted with roses and lavender and irises. In June it's in full bloom and essentially empty by 6pm. Start at Bastille, walk east, come back down through the Marais.
📍 Bastille, 12th arr · free · 4.7km elevated · best in morning or late afternoon
Montmartre's Hidden Stairways & Vineyards▼
Most tourists walk straight up the main tourist path to Sacré-Cœur. Instead: from Abbesses Métro, take Rue Lepic, then cut east through the vineyard path (Clos Montmartre), past Salvador Dalí's house, through the hidden garden of la Bonne Franquette, and up the back stairs to the top. You'll see nobody. The vineyard has been here since 1933 and produces real wine. Then catch the city view at 9:30pm. Walk back down through the charming streets of Abbesses.
📍 18th arr · start at Abbesses Métro · 2-hour wander · free · evening is best
Walk Every Bridge on the Seine — Make a Wish Game▼
There are 37 bridges across the Seine through Paris. The inner stretch from Pont d'Iéna to Pont de Bercy has about 20 — walkable in 3 hours. The game: at each bridge you each make a wish (silently or aloud, your call). The rule: the wish has to be different at each bridge. Start at Pont d'Iéna under the Eiffel Tower and walk east. Stop for wine at any point when you feel like it. This is a good way to see Paris at street level without a plan.
📍 Pont d'Iéna east · 3–4 hours · free · best started at 6pm
Belleville Street Art Walk▼
Belleville (20th arr) is Paris's most concentrated street art neighborhood — murals on every surface, commissioned works from international artists, independent galleries in old workshops. Start at Couronnes Métro and walk north up Boulevard de Belleville, turning into the side streets. The Cour Damoye (Bastille side) and the Oberkampf corridor have the best concentration. Follow it with dinner at one of the Vietnamese or Chinese restaurants on the boulevard — Belleville is also Paris's best Asian food neighborhood.
📍 20th arr · Couronnes Métro · 2-hour walk · free · go afternoon or evening
Père Lachaise at Golden Hour▼
Paris's most famous cemetery is also one of its most beautiful parks — 44 hectares of winding cobblestone paths, elaborate 19th-century monuments, enormous old trees, and the graves of Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Édith Piaf, Marcel Proust, Frédéric Chopin. Go at 5–6pm on a weekday: quiet, golden light filtering through the trees, cats sleeping on tombstones. Oscar Wilde's grave has been re-glassed (people used to kiss it lipstick-covered). Hauntingly beautiful and not morbid at all.
📍 20th arr · Père Lachaise Métro · free · closes at 6pm · go 4–6pm
Latin Quarter's Medieval Streets at Night▼
The 5th and 6th arrondissements at night are completely different from daytime. Start at Place Maubert, walk up Rue de la Montagne Sainte-Geneviève past the 6th-century origins of Paris, through Rue Mouffetard (oldest market street in Paris, still cobbled), around the Panthéon, through the back streets to Boulevard Saint-Germain. Stop at a terrace for wine. These streets are basically unchanged since the Middle Ages. Best done 9–11pm when tourists are gone and the locals are out.
📍 5th–6th arr · Place Maubert Métro · 2-hour walk · best 9–11pm
Canal Saint-Martin at Dusk▼
The Canal Saint-Martin (10th arr) is Paris's coolest neighborhood waterway — iron footbridges, plane trees, locks, and the most atmospheric evening light in the city. Walk from République north toward Jaurès as the sun goes down (around 9:45pm in June). Stop at any of the dozens of restaurants and bars along the canal — Chez Prune, Du Pain et des Idées (get their pistachio-chocolate croissant earlier in the day), or one of the outdoor terrace restaurants. The whole area feels like a village inside the city.
📍 10th arr · République Métro · 2km canal walk · best 8–10pm in summer
Butte-aux-Cailles — The Hidden Village▼
In the 13th arrondissement, below Place d'Italie, is a neighborhood that almost no tourist visits — cobblestone streets, low houses, a village square, street art, and independent bars that feel like someone's living room. The Butte-aux-Cailles has been a working-class and bohemian neighborhood since the 19th century. Walk through at 8pm, stop at La Folie en Tête (bar, bookshop, music) or Les Cailloux (Italian). You'll think you found a secret, and you basically did.
📍 13th arr · Corvisart Métro · free · evening walk · 1.5 hours
Night Walk to See Paris Illuminated▼
Paris lights its monuments at night from dusk to 1am. Plan a route: start at Trocadéro (Eiffel Tower view), walk across Pont d'Iéna to the tower base, then east along the Seine (Pont Alexandre III, Grand Palais) toward Notre-Dame, over to the islands and home. The Eiffel Tower sparkles for 5 minutes every hour on the hour until 1am. Position yourself on Pont d'Iéna or Pont de Bir-Hakeim for the sparkle at midnight — it's impossible not to be moved by it.
📍 Trocadéro to Île Saint-Louis · 5km walk · sparkle on the hour · best at midnight
Explore the 7th — Bourgeois Paris at Its Finest▼
The 7th arrondissement is the quietest, grandest, most beautiful residential neighborhood in Paris. Walk: from Musée Rodin's sculpture garden through the quiet streets of the Invalides, down Rue Saint-Dominique (the best street market in Paris), through the Champ de Mars, back past the Eiffel Tower. The 7th has no tourist attractions except the Rodin and the tower — it's just beautiful streets, well-dressed Parisians, and the best bread shops in the city. Walk it at 6pm.
📍 7th arr · Varenne Métro · 2–3 hour walk · free · beautiful any time of day
Saint-Germain Book Shopping at Shakespeare & Co▼
37 Rue de la Bûcherie, 5th — the most famous English-language bookshop in the world, directly across from Notre-Dame. Go in the late afternoon, give yourselves 45 minutes each to find one book for the other person — pick it based on what you know about them, or what you think they need. Pay for each other's book. Walk out, sit on the quai across from Notre-Dame, and read the first page of the book you were given. Hemingway would approve.
📍 37 Rue de la Bûcherie, 5th · free entry · open daily until 10pm · ~€15/book
Sunrise Walk to the Tip of Île de la Cité▼
The very western point of Île de la Cité — Square du Vert-Galant — is a triangular garden at water level beneath the Pont Neuf. At 5:45am in June you'll be completely alone there. The city is waking up. The river is silver. Bring two cups of coffee from a 24-hour café on the Île Saint-Louis. The Vert-Galant at dawn feels like standing at the prow of a ship sailing through Paris. One of those things you do once and never forget.
📍 Square du Vert-Galant · western tip of Île de la Cité · free · go at 5:30am in June
Palais Royal Gardens at Night▼
The Palais Royal (1st arr) is one of the most beautiful spaces in Paris and almost nobody is there after 9pm. The long colonnade gardens, the 18th-century arcades, Daniel Buren's striped columns in the courtyard (iconic Paris art), the quiet central fountain, the restaurants tucked into the arcade. In June you can sit in the garden until the closing bell at 11pm. Walk in from the Rue de Rivoli entrance. Have champagne from the café Le Grand Véfour and pretend this is normal life.
📍 Palais Royal gardens · 1st arr · free · open until 11pm in summer

Vedettes du Pont Neuf — Private Evening Boat Cruise▼
A 1-hour guided boat cruise through central Paris on a small, open boat — better than Bateaux Mouches, which are floating tourist buses. Vedettes du Pont Neuf departs from Square du Vert-Galant (the western tip of Île de la Cité) — steps from Megi's neighborhood. Classical Concert Inside Sainte-Chapelle▼
Sainte-Chapelle (4 Boulevard du Palais, 4th) is a 13th-century Gothic chapel whose upper nave is entirely made of stained glass — 1,113 panels of it, 15 windows, 50 feet high. Evening chamber music concerts run here year-round: Vivaldi Four Seasons, Mozart, Handel. Book tickets at classictic.com or billetréduc.com. Concerts run 60–75 minutes, no intermission. Sitting inside that room as music echoes through the glass is genuinely overwhelming. ~€35pp.
📍 4 Blvd du Palais, 4th · classictic.com · ~€35pp · evenings year-round
Eiffel Tower Midnight Sparkle from Pont d'Iéna▼
Position yourselves on Pont d'Iéna (or Pont de Bir-Hakeim, slightly further for a more dramatic angle) before midnight. The Eiffel Tower sparkles for 5 minutes on the hour, every hour, until 1am. At midnight, with the whole city behind you and the river below you, the sparkle is something else. It's 20,000 lightbulbs. Get there 10 minutes early. Bring a glass of wine in a to-go cup from a nearby café. This is the quintessential Paris moment and it still works.
📍 Pont d'Iéna or Pont de Bir-Hakeim · free · sparkle at midnight · plan to arrive 11:50pm
Musée Rodin at Dusk — Sculpture Garden▼
The Musée Rodin (77 Rue de Varenne, 7th) has one of the most beautiful garden museums in Europe — The Thinker in the courtyard, The Gates of Hell at the back, The Burghers of Calais on the lawn. The garden closes at 6:30pm but admission is €7 (or €1 for the garden alone). Go at 5pm on a Friday: the late afternoon light on the bronze sculptures is extraordinary and there are almost no visitors. Then walk the 7th arrondissement as Paris starts its evening.
📍 77 Rue de Varenne, 7th · €13 museum, €4 garden only · Tue–Sun · close 6:30pm

Rooftop View at Institut du Monde Arabe▼
The Institut du Monde Arabe (1 Rue des Fossés Saint-Bernard, 5th) has an extraordinary rooftop terrace restaurant — Le Zyriab — with a 180-degree view over the Seine, Notre-Dame, and the Île Saint-Louis. Private Picnic on Champ de Mars — Eiffel Tower at Night▼
The grass of Champ de Mars facing the Eiffel Tower is filled with people all evening in summer — tourists and Parisians both, drinking wine, having picnics, watching the tower. Join them. Build a spread: grab wine from Nicolas, bread from a boulangerie, vegan spread or hummus, fruit. Spread a blanket at the central axis and face the tower. The first sparkle after 10pm is at 10pm exactly. Stay for the midnight one. Paris does not do this better anywhere else.

Afternoon Tea at Angelina's▼
226 Rue de Rivoli, 1st — the Tuileries gardens on one side, Paris on the other. Angelina's is famous for its African hot chocolate (the richest, thickest, most serious hot chocolate in the world) and its Mont-Blanc pastry. The interior is 1903 Belle Époque — painted ceilings, gilt mirrors, marble tables. Go on a rainy afternoon or just because. The sorbet options are vegan; most pastries aren't but a few are (ask the server). A 90-minute sit here will reset your whole day.

Book a Suite at Hôtel du Jeu de Paume — on the Island▼
54 Rue Saint-Louis en l'Île — the best hotel on Île Saint-Louis, in a 17th-century royal tennis court (jeu de paume). The suites are extraordinary: exposed timber, stone walls, river views, a private courtyard. If you're staying here June 3–12 anyway (as recommended), upgrade to the suite category for one night. Order Berthillon sorbet room service from across the street. This is not extravagant — this is the whole point of being in Paris.
📍 54 Rue Saint-Louis en l'Île, 4th · jeudepaumehotel.com · suites from ~€450/night
See a Film at MK2 Quai de Seine — Floating Cinema▼
MK2 Quai de Seine (14 Quai de la Seine, 19th) is a cinema complex on both banks of the canal, connected by a small boat. The boat is free for ticket holders. In summer they run rooftop open-air screenings. Even for a regular film — check what's showing, the French screen everything in VO (version originale with subtitles) — the experience of taking a little canal boat to see a movie and then sitting on the canal afterward is genuinely lovely.
📍 14 Quai de la Seine, 19th · mk2.com · ~€12pp · rooftop screenings in summer
Sunrise at the Sacré-Cœur Steps▼
Take the Métro at 5am to Abbesses and walk up to Sacré-Cœur for the 5:45am sunrise. The entire city of Paris turns pink and then gold below you. You'll be alone or nearly so. The basilica itself opens at 6am. This requires willingness to lose one night of sleep, which is absolutely worth it. Bring coffee in a thermos, a blanket, and something to eat. The climb down through the waking neighborhood afterward — boulangeries opening, street sweepers, first café chairs going out — is its own reward.
📍 Sacré-Cœur steps, 18th · sunrise 5:45am in June · Abbesses Métro · free · bring coffee
Buy Something Together at the Bouquinistes▼
The bouquinistes are the green-box booksellers who've lined the Seine quais since the 16th century — 900 of them, 3km of vintage books, prints, postcards, and posters. Find one thing together: a vintage Paris print, an old book in French even if you can't read it, an antique map of the neighborhood. Spend €15–30. This is your shared object from this trip. Wrap it, carry it home, frame it. The quai between Pont Saint-Michel and Pont de l'Alma has the best selection.

Horse-Drawn Carriage in the Bois de Boulogne▼
The Bois de Boulogne (16th arr) has horse-drawn carriage rides available on weekend afternoons — through the wooded paths of Paris's western park, past the lakes, through the allées that have been a Parisian leisure tradition since Napoleon III. This is unabashedly romantic and old-fashioned and worth doing once. Book through the carriage operators at the park entrance near Porte Dauphine. Follow with dinner in the 16th or back in the Marais.
📍 Bois de Boulogne, 16th · Porte Dauphine entrance · weekends · ~€45 for 30 min
Late Night Ice Cream on the Quai▼
This is the simplest one on the list. Get Berthillon sorbet (vegan: cassis, lemon, mango, passion fruit) from the original shop at 31 Rue Saint-Louis en l'Île before it closes at 10pm. Walk to the steps on Quai de Béthune — the southern quai of Île Saint-Louis, below street level, facing the river and the Left Bank. Sit. Eat. Watch the river. This is what you came for. Don't overthink it.
📍 Berthillon + Quai de Béthune, 4th · ~€4 · no planning required · any warm night
Look for Our Swan — Square du Vert-Galant at Dusk▼
Square du Vert-Galant is the triangular garden at the very western tip of Île de la Cité — below the Pont Neuf, at water level, the prow of an island sailing through the heart of Paris. In the evenings, swans drift along this stretch of the Seine. The ritual is simple: go at dusk, sit on the stone edge facing the river, and wait for your swan to appear. You'll know it's yours. The Seine funnels here, the current splits around the point, and sometimes a swan comes through alone. If it comes to you — that's the one. This is not a planned activity. It's the kind of thing that either happens or doesn't, and either way you've spent 45 minutes on the most beautiful river perch in Paris as the city goes gold around you.
📍 Square du Vert-Galant, western tip of Île de la Cité · free · go at dusk (9pm in June) · bring wine
Louvre After Hours — Friday Late Night▼
The Louvre opens until 9:45pm on Fridays and Saturdays — crowds thin dramatically after 6pm. Pick one wing only (trying to see the whole Louvre is a mistake). Go to the Richelieu wing (French paintings) or the Denon wing (Italian sculpture, Venus de Milo) and take your time. No queue to see anything. The Winged Victory of Samothrace at the top of the Daru staircase with almost no one around is astonishing. Finish at the Pyramid looking up at the Louvre lit from inside.
📍 Louvre · Fri/Sat until 9:45pm · €22pp · arrive at 6pm · one wing only
🎭 Arts, Music & Culture
Jazz at Le Caveau de la Huchette▼
5 Rue de la Huchette, 5th — a jazz club in a 1940s stone vault. Louis Armstrong played here. Doors open at 8pm, €15–20 entry, live jazz from 9:30pm. The vault is low-ceilinged, the dance floor is small and sticky, the bar is cheap, and the musicians are genuinely excellent. It's one of the only places in Paris where you can still swing dance to a live band at midnight. Go after your Seine picnic — it's a 5-minute walk from the river.

New Morning Jazz Club▼
7-9 Rue des Petites Écuries, 10th — Paris's premier jazz club, serious room, international acts. New Morning has hosted Miles Davis, Chet Baker, Stan Getz. The current programming is strong: African jazz, Afro-Cuban, soul, contemporary jazz. Check their calendar at newmorning.com and book the night that has the best lineup. €20–30pp. Doors at 7:30pm, shows at 8:30pm. Atelier des Lumières — Immersive Art▼
38 Rue Saint-Maur, 11th — a former iron foundry converted into an immersive digital art space where paintings are projected floor to ceiling across vast walls to a curated soundtrack. Current programs change seasonally — past shows: Van Gogh, Klimt, Cézanne. The space is extraordinary: 3,300 sqm of projection surface, 140 video projectors. You walk through the artwork. Opera at Palais Garnier — Guided Tour or Evening Show▼
Place de l'Opéra, 9th — one of the most opulent buildings in Europe. The 1875 Grand Staircase, the Marc Chagall painted ceiling above the auditorium (Paris's most surprising ceiling), the mirrored Grand Foyer that puts Versailles to shame. If tickets for a performance are too expensive (€30–200), the guided tour alone is worth it — €15, daily, no show required. Musée de l'Orangerie — Monet's Water Lilies▼
Jardin des Tuileries, 1st — a small museum containing two oval rooms purpose-built by Monet to display his Water Lilies triptych. You enter the oval room, sit on the curved bench in the center, and the paintings surround you 360 degrees. They are enormous. The light comes from above through a diffused skylight. This is one of the most significant art experiences in Europe and it's consistently underrated. Book ahead, go on a weekday, and budget 30 minutes just for those two rooms.

Crazy Horse Paris — The Classic Cabaret▼

Centre Pompidou Evening — Modern Art & Rooftop▼
Place Georges-Pompidou, 4th — the great inside-out building of Paris. The permanent collection (floors 4–5) is one of the best modern art museums in the world. Go if she's up for it; it's one of Paris's best kept secrets from the tourist world and is pure city.
📍 5 Blvd Poissonnière, 2nd · Fri/Sat · opens midnight · €15–20 entry · rexclub.com
Oberkampf Bar Hop — Paris's Best Street for Bars▼
Rue Oberkampf (11th arr) is Paris's best bar street — organic, local, genuinely fun. Start at Aux Deux Amis (45 Rue Oberkampf) for natural wine and small plates at 8pm. Move to Le Supercoin (3 Rue Baudelique — actually a sister street, Montmartre side) or Café Charbon (109 Rue Oberkampf), an 1880s brasserie with live music and a young crowd. The street stays alive until 2am on weekends. Just walk and go in wherever looks good. That's all you need.
📍 Rue Oberkampf, 11th · no plan needed · 8pm–2am · ~€40–60pp for the whole evening
After-Midnight Quai de Montebello — The Best View Nobody Knows▼
Quai de Montebello (5th arr, Left Bank) is the quai directly across the river from Notre-Dame's apse. After midnight, the bouquiniste boxes are closed, the tourists are gone, the cathedral is lit, and the water reflects it all. Walk there from anywhere after a late dinner or bar. Bring a bottle of something. Sit on the low stone wall with your legs hanging over the river. This is not a bar, not a restaurant, not an activity. It's just Paris being Paris when everyone else has gone to bed.
📍 Quai de Montebello, 5th · free · best after midnight · Notre-Dame lit until 1am
Le Silencio — David Lynch's Private Club▼
142 Rue Montmartre, 2nd — designed by David Lynch as a private club, Le Silencio opens to the public after midnight on weekends. The space is extraordinary: three levels of red velvet, strange sculpture, a live music stage, film projections, and a bar stocked with things you won't find elsewhere. Named after the Club Silencio in Mulholland Drive. Dress well. Get there at 12:30am. €20 entry. The crowd is fashion, film, and music people. Completely unlike anything else in Paris.
📍 142 Rue Montmartre, 2nd · public after midnight Fri/Sat · €20 entry · dress up
Midnight Crêpe Stand Walk▼
Several crêpe stands in the Latin Quarter and near the Marais run until 2–3am — particularly on Rue de la Huchette and around Bastille. A buckwheat galette with jam or a classic sugar-and-butter crêpe is €3–5. Walk and eat. This is the best late-night food in Paris that doesn't require sitting down or planning. Nutella crêpes are not traditional and not recommended; butter and sugar or jam is the move. Walk back to the island along the Seine after.
📍 Rue de la Huchette, 5th + near Bastille · open until 2–3am · €3–5 per crêpe
Pigalle & SoPi Late-Night Bar Scene▼
South Pigalle (SoPi) — the blocks south of Pigalle Métro — is Paris's most dynamic nightlife neighborhood right now. Walk Rue Frochot, Rue Henry Monnier, and Rue Notre-Dame de Lorette. Bars include Dirty Dick (tiki bar, genuinely excellent), La Nouvelle Seine (river bar, on a barge), Hardware Société (cocktails), and half a dozen natural wine bars. The area peaks at 10pm–1am. Best on a Thursday, Friday, or Saturday. No plan — just walk and look.
📍 South Pigalle, 9th arr · Pigalle Métro · 9pm–2am · no plan needed · walk and explore
3am Onion Soup at a Brasserie des Halles▼
The brasseries around Les Halles (1st arr) — Au Pied de Cochon chief among them, open 24/7 since 1947 — serve the classic 3am onion soup (gratinée des Halles) that Parisians have been eating after a night out since the 19th century. It's deeply un-vegan (cheese, beef broth, the whole thing). But it's a Paris rite of passage and Megi can order the French onion with vegetable broth (ask: "bouillon de légumes"). Best eaten slightly drunk and very hungry at 3am.
📍 Au Pied de Cochon · 6 Rue Coquillière, 1st · open 24/7 · onion soup ~€18 · iconic
Japanese Whisky Bar — Bar Benfiddich Style▼
Paris has excellent Japanese whisky bars — Sherry Butt (20 Rue Beautrellis, 4th, Marais) is one of the best whisky bars in the city, stocks serious Japanese bottles (Hibiki, Yamazaki, Hakushu, Karuizawa), and has a focused cocktail menu. Small, quiet, serious. Go at 10pm when it's full but not overwhelming. If you want to go deeper: Bar Hemingway at the Ritz (much more expensive, €35/drink) does classic Japanese whisky service in a legendary literary setting.
📍 Sherry Butt · 20 Rue Beautrellis, 4th · 7pm–2am · whisky from €15 · Marais · no res
Pont de Bir-Hakeim at 1am — Paris's Best Bridge▼
The two-level 1905 bridge at Bir-Hakeim (15th/16th arr) — the elevated Métro runs along the top, the foot bridge below — was used in Inception and Last Tango in Paris. At 1am, cross on the lower pedestrian level: the Eiffel Tower is directly in front of you, the river below, Métro tracks above. The proportions are extraordinary. This is a 10-minute detour from anywhere in the western city and it's worth every minute. Walk it once each direction, slowly.
📍 Pont de Bir-Hakeim · 15th/16th · free · best at 1am · Eiffel Tower dead ahead
Night Photography Session — 10pm to Midnight▼
Paris at night is designed to be photographed. Give yourselves 2 hours and a route: Pont d'Iéna (tower reflection in the river) → Pont Alexandre III (gold lamps, gilded statues) → Place de la Concorde obelisk → Tuileries garden lit at night → Louvre pyramid. Use portrait mode on your phones for each other. Paris at 10–11pm is quieter than you expect, and the light on the monuments at that hour is better than any time of day. You'll end up with 300 photos and keep maybe 20 great ones.
📍 7th → 8th → 1st arr · 10pm–midnight · just phones · free · 5km walking route
🚆 Day Escapes
Giverny — Monet's Garden in June▼
Claude Monet's house and garden in Giverny (Normandy) — the Japanese bridge, the water lilies, the wisteria, the flower gardens that are painted across 250 canvases at the Orangerie and Musée d'Orsay. June is the best month to visit: everything is in bloom. Take the train from Gare Saint-Lazare to Vernon (75 min), then a bus or taxi 5km to Giverny. The garden opens at 9:30am; arrive early to beat the crowds. Pack a picnic. The apple orchards behind the house are perfect in June.
📍 Giverny, Normandy · train from Saint-Lazare + bus · €12 garden entry · go early June
Versailles Gardens — Picnic in the Grand Trianon Park▼
Everyone goes to Versailles for the château. Skip the château lines and buy a garden-only ticket (€9) — you get the full 800 hectares of Le Nôtre's geometry, the Grand Canal, the fountains, the bosquets (wooded rooms). Walk to the Grand Trianon (Marie Antoinette's private retreat) and past it to the Hameau de la Reine — her fake farm village. Bring a picnic, find a spot by the canal, stay for 5 hours. On Tuesday/Saturday/Sunday the "Grandes Eaux" fountains run — add €10. Come back exhausted.
📍 Versailles · RER C from Paris · garden-only €9 · Grandes Eaux €10 extra · go Tue/Sat/Sun
Épernay — Champagne Cellars & Picnic▼
90 minutes by train from Gare de l'Est, Épernay is the capital of Champagne — the Avenue de Champagne is 1km of Champagne house façades (Moët & Chandon, Perrier-Jouët, Pol Roger) with miles of chalk caves underneath. Book a cave tour + tasting at Moët (the biggest, most organized) or de Castellane (more intimate, better views). Then picnic in the vineyard with a bottle you bought at cost. A full day trip: train, caves, tasting, lunch, train back. €60–90pp all in.
📍 Épernay · train from Gare de l'Est · 90 min · cave tours €30pp · full day
Fontainebleau Forest — Rock Climbing & Château▼
45 minutes by train from Gare de Lyon — the Fontainebleau forest is where professional climbers train on the sandstone boulders (bouldering at Bleau is world-famous). You don't need to be a climber: the forest is extraordinary for walking, and the Château de Fontainebleau (a royal residence from the 12th century, Napoleon's favorite) is better than Versailles and half the crowds. Pack a picnic. Walk the forest paths in the morning, château in the afternoon. Back in Paris by 6pm.
📍 Fontainebleau · train from Gare de Lyon · 45 min · château €14pp · forest free
Provins — Medieval UNESCO Village▼
90 minutes from Gare de l'Est (one train, no connection), Provins is a remarkably preserved 12th-century medieval trading town — ramparts, towers, underground passages, and rose gardens (it's the city of roses). Almost no tourists. The rampart walk is extraordinary and the underground chalk caves can be toured. On weekends in summer they run medieval jousting and falconry shows in the upper town. Lunch at a terrace restaurant under the tower. Back in Paris by 7pm.
📍 Provins · train from Gare de l'Est · 90 min · ramparts free, caves €8pp · UNESCO site
🛍 Markets & Spontaneous
Marché d'Aligre Sunday Morning▼
Place d'Aligre, 12th — the best food market in Paris and the most local. A covered hall (Beauvau market) plus an outdoor street market: vegetables and fruit piled high, olive stands, spice dealers, fresh bread, North African vendors, and one of the best cheese selections in the city. Go Sunday at 9am before it gets crowded. Shop for the day's picnic: tomatoes, herbs, peaches (in June), hummus, good olives. Le Baron Rouge (the wine barrel bar) is next door and opens at 10am Sunday.
📍 Place d'Aligre, 12th · open Tue–Sun mornings until noon · free entry · best on Sunday
Marché aux Puces — Flea Market at Saint-Ouen▼
Porte de Clignancourt, 18th — the world's largest antique market. 2,000 vendors across 14 separate markets covering 7 hectares. You can spend 4 hours and not see it all. Go on Saturday morning (it opens at 9am). The Marché Paul Bert and Serpette sections have the best 20th-century design and furniture. The outer stalls have cheaper vintage clothing, records, and oddities. Haggling is normal and expected. Budget whatever you feel like; bring cash.
📍 Porte de Clignancourt, 18th · Sat/Sun/Mon · opens 9am · cash preferred · free entry
Île de la Cité Flower Market — Walk & Buy Something Living▼
Place Louis Lépine, 4th — a permanent covered flower market on the Île de la Cité, steps from Notre-Dame, open Monday through Saturday. On Sundays it becomes a bird market (Marché aux Oiseaux). Go on a Saturday morning and buy her flowers from the market — let her pick exactly what she wants from the stalls, then buy them. Walk back to the island together. This is a 45-minute activity but feels like the whole morning. Then coffee at a café on the island.
📍 Place Louis Lépine, 4th · Mon–Sat flower market · Sun bird market · 8am–7pm · free entry
Say Nothing — Just Walk. Let Paris Decide.▼
Show up at her door, say nothing, just start walking. No destination. No phone navigation. Turn whenever you feel like it. If something smells good, go in. If there's a courtyard, look through the gate. If there's music, follow it. If you find a good-looking bar, stop. Paris is dense enough that you will never run out of things to discover within 10 minutes of Île Saint-Louis in any direction. The best nights often have no plan. The city will fill in the rest.
📍 Start: her door · destination: unknown · cost: whatever you spend · best on warm evenings
Buy a Perfect Single Thing Together▼
Walk the bouquinistes or the Saint-Ouen flea market with one rule: you must collectively agree on one perfect object to buy together — a vintage Paris map, an old French novel, a postcard from 1920, a print, a single perfect glass from a brocante stall. Budget €15–40. The hunt is the thing — arguing about whether something is worth it, what you'll do with it, who keeps it if things go wrong. If things go right, it becomes an artifact of this trip. Frame it when you get home.
📍 Bouquinistes on the Seine or Saint-Ouen · any morning · ~€15–40 budget · just one thing

Essential Tips

🚇 Getting Around

Buy a Paris Visite card or use the Navigo Découverte weekly pass (€26, buy at any metro station with a photo). Covers all zones including RER to airports. Taxis/Uber for late nights and Orly.

📱 Apps to Download

Bonjour RATP (metro maps + times), Google Maps offline (download Paris map), La Fourchette/TheFork (restaurant reservations), XE Currency, and Duolingo for a few French basics (locals genuinely appreciate the effort).

💳 Money

Most places accept Visa/Mastercard. Always carry €50–100 cash — several of the best restaurants (Ma Cuisine, Le Baron Rouge) are cash-only. Withdraw from ATMs inside bank branches, not standalone machines.

🍷 Wine Buying in Burgundy

You can bring 5 litres of wine back to the US duty-free as a returning resident. Pack bubble wrap and check a dedicated wine bag. The Époisses cheese: wrap in multiple layers, keep cool, declare it (soft ripened cheese is fine to bring home).

🏨 Hotel Booking — Urgent

Hôtel du Jeu de Paume is the top pick for June 3–12. Book direct at jeudepaumehotel.com — direct rates are typically better than OTAs. Also consider Hôtel L de Lutèce (paris-hotel-lutece.com) as backup. Both on Île Saint-Louis.

🍽 Reservations to Make Now

Book Septime, Saturne, Frenchie, Le Sergent Recruteur, and Le Benaton (Beaune) immediately — all book weeks out. Le Baron Rouge, Chez Janou, and Brasserie de l'Isle Saint-Louis are walk-in.
