Saturday, May 24
Arrive Paris · Canal Saint-Martin
📍 12th arr · Hotel Locomo
⛅68°F · Partly cloudy

Day Plan

  • Land at Orly — take G7 taxi straight to Hotel Locomo (Gare de Lyon/12th arr, ~35 min, €35–45). Skip the train today, you're tired.
  • Check in, drop bags, take a hot shower. Resist the urge to sleep — push through to evening and you'll beat jet lag.
  • Slow afternoon walk around Canal Saint-Martin (10th arr, 20-min walk or quick metro). Iron bridges, green water, laid-back locals. This is the real Paris energy.
  • Pick up provisions at any neighborhood market for tomorrow morning.

Restaurants

Lunch
Du Pain et des Idées
34 rue Yves Toudic, 10th · Paris's most famous bakery. Get the escargot pastry and a tartine. Go early — it sells out. Closed Sunday.
Dinner
Virtus
29 rue de Cotte, 12th · Michelin-starred, right near your hotel. Contemporary French-Japanese, precision cooking. Book ahead.
Alt Dinner
Le Barav
6 rue Charles-François Dupuis, 3rd · Natural wine bar in the Marais. Small plates, great bottles, relaxed energy. Perfect for a tired first night.
03
Sunday, May 25
Hidden Museums · Marais & Montmartre
📍 3rd arr · Marais · 18th arr
⛅67°F · Light clouds

Day Plan

  • Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature (60 rue des Archives, 3rd) — a cabinet of curiosities centered on hunting. Bizarre taxidermy, Old Masters, surrealist installations. Zero tourists. One of Paris's weirdest, most wonderful rooms.
  • Walk through the Marais — browse the galleries on rue Vieille du Temple, grab a coffee.
  • Afternoon: Musée Gustave Moreau (14 rue de La Rochefoucauld, 9th) — symbolist painter's private studio, preserved exactly as he left it. Otherworldly paintings floor to ceiling. Almost always quiet.
  • Evening aperitif in the neighborhood before dinner.

Restaurants

Lunch
Chez Janou
2 rue Roger Verlomme, 3rd · Packed Provençal bistro in the Marais. 100+ pastis options, great ratatouille, very French atmosphere. Arrive at 12 or 12:15 or you'll wait.
Dinner
Septime
80 rue de Charonne, 11th · One of Paris's best — produce-driven bistronomie, inventive, soulful. Book 3–4 weeks ahead online. Worth it.
Alt Dinner
Le Barav
6 rue Charles-François Dupuis, 3rd · Great natural wine selection, snacky small plates. If Septime is booked, this is a perfect low-key night.
04
Monday, May 26
Promenade Plantée · Marché d'Aligre
📍 12th arr · Viaduc des Arts
🌤70°F · Mostly sunny

Day Plan

  • Morning: Marché d'Aligre (place d'Aligre, 12th) — the best, most local market in Paris. Skip the tourist markets. Outdoor flea section + covered food hall + wine bar at the corner. Go before noon.
  • Promenade Plantée — Paris's elevated park, built 1993, 4.5 km on a converted rail viaduct. Starts at Avenue Daumesnil, steps from your hotel. Walk east through the hanging gardens.
  • Below the promenade: Viaduc des Arts — artisan workshops built into the brick arches. Furniture restorers, violin makers, jewelers. Worth ducking into a few.
  • Late afternoon: rest at hotel or wander the quartier.

Restaurants

Lunch
Le Baron Rouge
1 rue Théophile Roussel, 12th · Beloved wine bar right at Marché d'Aligre. Oysters on the sidewalk, cheap house wine, charcuterie. Iconic local spot. Cash only, standing room, perfect.
Dinner
Passerini
65 rue Traversière, 12th · Italian-French from Roman chef Giovanni Passerini. Beautiful pastas, great natural wine list. One of the best Italians in Paris, 5 minutes from Hotel Locomo.
Alt Dinner
À la Biche au Bois
45 avenue Ledru-Rollin, 12th · Old-school French bistro, game dishes, cassoulet, classic fixed-price menu. The kind of place Paris grandmothers have eaten at for 40 years.
05
Tuesday, May 27
Musée des Arts et Métiers · Butte-aux-Cailles
📍 3rd arr · 13th arr
🌦66°F · Possible shower

Day Plan

  • Musée des Arts et Métiers (60 rue Réaumur, 3rd) — proto-steampunk museum of science, industry and invention, built inside a 12th-century priory. Foucault's original pendulum hangs from the church ceiling. Mechanical automata, early flying machines, Lavoisier's lab. Most underrated museum in Paris.
  • Walk through the République neighborhood, grab a coffee on one of the terraces.
  • Afternoon: Butte-aux-Cailles (13th arr, 25-min metro) — a village-within-the-city. Cobblestones, street murals, intimate bars. Completely off the tourist radar. Walk every street — it takes 20 minutes to cover the whole neighborhood.

Restaurants

Lunch
Le Hangar
12 impasse Berthaud, 3rd · Neighborhood bistro steps from Musée des Arts et Métiers. Unpretentious, good food, mostly locals. Steak frites, crème brûlée — the classics, done right.
Dinner
Le Temps des Cerises
18 rue de la Butte-aux-Cailles, 13th · A worker-owned cooperative bistro since 1976. Convivial, loud, honest food. Order the tarte tatin. Feels like eating in someone's home.
Alt Dinner
Chez Gladines
30 rue des Cinq Diamants, 13th · Basque bar and restaurant in Butte-aux-Cailles. Huge salads, duck confit, jambon Basque, great prices. Extremely popular — arrive early or wait.

Wednesday, May 28
Day Trip — Fontainebleau
📍 Fontainebleau Forest · 45 min by train
☀️71°F · Clear

Day Plan

  • Train from Gare de Lyon → Fontainebleau-Avon (direct, ~40 min, Transilien Line R). Leave by 9:30 am.
  • Château de Fontainebleau — grander than it looks in photos, UNESCO World Heritage. 1,500 rooms. Napoleon's apartments are extraordinary. Almost no crowds compared to Versailles. 3–4 hour visit.
  • Walk the formal gardens and the canal. The forest surrounds everything — enormous, ancient oaks and sandstone boulders.
  • Return train to Paris, back by 6–7 pm.

Restaurants

Lunch
La Table des Maréchaux
In Fontainebleau town · Classic French brasserie steps from the château. White tablecloths, set menus, solid cooking. Or grab a picnic from the market and eat in the gardens.
Dinner
Le Train Bleu
Gare de Lyon, Paris · Belle Époque masterpiece right in your train station. Gilded ceilings, murals, the full experience. Touristy but genuinely spectacular for a special occasion dinner.
Alt Dinner
Ground Control
81 rue du Charolais, 12th · Massive covered food hall in a former postal warehouse, 5 min from Hotel Locomo. Multiple kitchens, bars, live events. Great for a relaxed post-day-trip evening.

Thursday, May 29
Belleville · Pack for Burgundy
📍 20th arr · Belleville
⛅69°F · Variable

Day Plan

  • Belleville (20th arr) — Paris's most genuinely multicultural neighborhood. Chinese community, Tunisian community, Jewish community, artists. Zero tourist infrastructure. Walk along rue de Belleville, explore the side streets.
  • Street art all over the neighborhood — look for the painted staircases and murals off rue Denoyez.
  • Afternoon: pack, rest. You're heading to Burgundy tomorrow morning — train from Gare de Lyon.
  • Last dinner in Paris I — make it count.

Restaurants

Lunch
Dong Huong
14 rue Louis Bonnet, 11th · Legendary Vietnamese in Belleville, open since 1980. The pho and bun bo hue are exceptional. Cheap, crowded, fantastic. Cash only.
Dinner
Saturne
17 rue Notre-Dame-des-Victoires, 2nd · Natural wine-forward restaurant with impeccable Nordic-French cooking. One of the best in Paris. Book weeks ahead. The tasting menu is worth every euro.
Alt Dinner
Frenchie
5 rue du Nil, 2nd · Gregory Marchand's flagship bistro. Market-driven menu, constantly changing, always brilliant. Small room, buzzy atmosphere. Book ahead.

Friday, May 30
Arrive Beaune · Hospices de Beaune
📍 Beaune · With Lena & Cyril
🌤67°F · Clear

Day Plan

  • Train: Gare de Lyon → Beaune (TGV, ~2 hrs). Depart 9 am, arrive ~11 am. Meet Lena and Cyril.
  • Hospices de Beaune (Hôtel-Dieu) — the 15th-century charity hospital with the most extraordinary polychrome tile roof in France. Not to be missed. Roger van der Weyden's Last Judgment inside.
  • Walk the old town — Beaune is fully walled and medieval. The ramparts, the wine merchant caves, the Saturday morning market (the best in Burgundy).
  • Evening: aperitif with your hosts, then dinner out.

Restaurants

Lunch
Ma Cuisine
Place Carnot, Beaune · The classic top-end Beaune bistro. Cash only. Extraordinary wine list at astonishing prices (direct from producers). Reservations essential. Closed weekend — call ahead.
Dinner
Loiseau des Vignes
31 rue Maufoux, Beaune · Bernard Loiseau's wine-country restaurant. Seven-course menu gourmand option. Cellar of extraordinary Burgundies. The coq au vin is definitive.
Alt Dinner
La Dilettante
Beaune · Small, inventive wine bar with Asian influences and natural wine producers. Refreshingly different from the classic Burgundian menus. Great for a low-key first night.
09
Saturday, May 31
Côte de Nuits Wine Route
📍 Gevrey-Chambertin · Clos de Vougeot · Nuits-Saint-Georges
☀️68°F · Sunny

Day Plan

  • Hire a local driver or rent a car — the wine route is best done with someone else driving. Lena and Cyril likely have contacts.
  • Gevrey-Chambertin — walk the vineyard lanes. Domaine Rossignol-Trapet does tastings and is one of the better smaller houses. The Chambertin Grand Cru vineyard itself is just a field — but standing in it is something else.
  • Clos de Vougeot — UNESCO-listed walled vineyard, 50 hectares of Grand Cru. Tour the interior of the château (the press room dates to the 12th century).
  • Stop in Nuits-Saint-Georges for a café and a walk. Saturday market in the morning.

Restaurants

Lunch
Café de la Paix — Nuits-Saint-Georges
Classic village brasserie in Nuits-Saint-Georges. Simple plat du jour, local wine, tables outside. Exactly what you want mid-vineyard-tour.
Dinner
Ma Cuisine — Beaune
If you couldn't get in Friday — try again Saturday. The wine list alone is worth the trip. Ask what the sommelier is drinking himself. Cash only, book ahead.
Alt Dinner
Caves Madeleine
8 rue du Faubourg Madeleine, Beaune · Wine bar and restaurant with rotating seasonal menu emphasizing natural wines. Intimate, excellent, more casual than Loiseau.
10
Sunday, June 1
Vézelay · Fontenay Abbey · Époisses
📍 Northern Burgundy · Morvan hills
⛅65°F · Mixed

Day Plan

  • Drive north (~1.5 hrs from Beaune). Vézelay — a hilltop UNESCO pilgrimage village with a Romanesque basilica that will stop you cold. The light inside at noon is extraordinary. Walk every lane — the village is tiny and perfect.
  • En route back: Fontenay Abbey (1118 AD) — one of the oldest, best-preserved Cistercian monasteries in Europe. Austere, enormous, completely humbling. Very few visitors.
  • Stop at Époisses village — buy the famous washed-rind cheese directly from the fromagerie (Fromagerie Berthaut). Wrap it well. It's the best cheese in France and it smells like it.

Restaurants

Lunch
Le Bougainville — Vézelay
Village restaurant at the top of Vézelay. Terrasse with views over the Morvan valley. Local Burgundian classics — jambon persillé, oeufs en meurette. Simple and excellent.
Dinner
Le Benaton
25 faubourg Bretonnière, Beaune · Michelin star since 2006. Contemporary Burgundian with bold, precise flavors. The tasting menu with wine pairings is the move. Book well in advance.
Alt Dinner
Loiseau des Vignes
31 rue Maufoux, Beaune · If Benaton is booked. The wine list here is one of the best in France. Seven courses, extraordinary Pinot Noirs, the full Burgundy experience.

Monday, June 2
Côte de Beaune · Return to Paris
📍 Meursault · Puligny-Montrachet → Paris
🌤68°F · Clearing

Day Plan

  • Morning drive: the southern wine villages — Pommard, Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet. Puligny is quieter and the whites are extraordinary. Stop at a domaine for a morning tasting.
  • Buxy cooperative cave (Caves des Vignerons de Buxy) — one of the best co-ops in Burgundy. Buy bottles direct. Great value on Montagny and Mercurey.
  • Farewells to Lena and Cyril. Train back to Paris Gare de Lyon (~2 hrs). Check into your Île Saint-Louis hotel.

Restaurants

Lunch

8 rue du Faubourg Madeleine · Last Burgundy meal. Wine bar format, fresh seasonal dishes, natural wines. A perfect send-off before the train back to Paris.
Dinner
Le Sergent Recruteur
41 rue Saint-Louis en l'Île, 4th · Michelin-starred restaurant right on your new island. Inventive contemporary French, excellent wine list. First dinner at your new base. Book ahead.
Alt Dinner
Sorza
51 rue Saint-Louis en l'Île, 4th · Italian-French fusion on the island. Elegant but relaxed. Good for a low-key first evening back in Paris if you're tired from travel.

Tuesday, June 3
Île Saint-Louis · Latin Quarter · Cluny
📍 4th arr · Île Saint-Louis
☀️72°F · Beautiful

Day Plan

  • Settle into your new hotel. Walk every street on the Île Saint-Louis — there are only a handful, and it takes 20 minutes. It's a village suspended in the Seine.
  • Berthillon — the legendary ice cream shop on the island. 31 rue Saint-Louis en l'Île. Get two scoops, eat on the quai, watch the river.
  • Afternoon: Musée de Cluny (5th arr, 5-min walk across Pont de la Tournelle) — medieval Paris, Roman baths underneath the building, and the Lady and the Unicorn tapestries — six panels, 15th century, absolutely devastating in their beauty.
  • Evening: walk the Quai de Bourbon as the sun sets over Notre-Dame. With a bottle of something you brought from Époisses if you're feeling it.

Restaurants

Lunch
Café Saint Régis
6 rue Jean du Bellay, 4th · The Île Saint-Louis café. Art Deco, great terrace, classic Paris energy. Croque monsieur, salads, kir. Settle in and watch the island go by.
Dinner
Le Sergent Recruteur
41 rue Saint-Louis en l'Île · Michelin star, tasting menu format. Contemporary, surprising. The best restaurant on the island. Book ahead.
Alt Dinner
Les Fous de l'Île
33 rue des Deux Ponts, 4th · Neighbourhood bistro on the island. Simple, good, very local. Great for a first night when you want something easy and close.

Wednesday, June 4
Château de Vincennes · Bois de Vincennes
📍 12th/Vincennes · East Paris
☀️74°F · Clear & warm

Day Plan

  • Château de Vincennes — a full medieval royal fortress east of Paris that 95% of tourists never visit. Perfectly preserved 14th-century dungeon keep, royal chapel, moat. Take metro Line 1 to Château de Vincennes.
  • The Bois de Vincennes surrounds it — Paris's largest park, lake, rowing boats, cycling paths. Vastly less crowded than the Bois de Boulogne. Pack a picnic from the market on the island.
  • Stop at the Parc Floral inside the Bois — free or cheap entry, a botanical garden with an outdoor concert stage (often jazz in June).

Restaurants

Lunch
Picnic in Bois de Vincennes
Grab charcuterie, cheese, bread, and wine from Marché d'Aligre (nearby) or a local shop on the island. Eat on the grass by the lake. Best lunch format possible on a warm day.
Dinner
Au Bougnat
26 rue Chanoinesse, 4th · Authentic old bistro steps from Notre-Dame. Rustic, traditional, great value. One of the few real neighborhood restaurants left this close to the cathedral.
Alt Dinner
Sorza
51 rue Saint-Louis en l'Île · Elegant Italian-French on the island. Good pastas, excellent wine list. Easy and close to home for a quiet Wednesday night.

Thursday, June 5
Les Puces de Saint-Ouen
📍 Saint-Ouen · World's Largest Antique Market
🌤72°F · Partly cloudy

Day Plan

  • Metro to Garibaldi or Porte de Clignancourt. Arrive before 11 am — dealers source here early, and the best stuff goes fast.
  • Les Puces de Saint-Ouen — 2,500+ stalls across 15 named markets. Marché Biron for serious antiques and art. Marché Dauphine for vintage fashion, records, books. Marché Paul Bert for furniture and mid-century.
  • Budget 3–4 hours minimum. Negotiate everything. Do not pay first ask. Bring cash.
  • Afternoon return to Paris. Rest or explore the island.

Restaurants

Lunch
Chez Louisette
Inside Marché Vernaison, Les Puces · Accordion, singing waiters, Edith Piaf songs, red-checked tablecloths. Deeply theatrical and completely wonderful. Get the pot-au-feu. A Paris experience you won't forget.
Dinner
Chez Janou
2 rue Roger Verlomme, 3rd · Provençal bistro in the Marais. If you haven't been yet — go. 100+ pastis varieties, great food, always packed, always fun.
Alt Dinner
Brasserie de l'Isle Saint-Louis
55 quai de Bourbon, 4th · Classic French brasserie right on the island's northern quai. Choucroute, steak frites, cold Alsatian beer. River views. Open late.

Friday, June 6
Jardins Albert Kahn · Piscine Molitor
📍 Boulogne-Billancourt · West Paris
☀️75°F · Warm & sunny

Day Plan

  • Jardins Albert Kahn (Boulogne-Billancourt, 20-min metro) — one of Paris's most beautiful and least-known gardens. French orchards, English rose garden, Japanese forest garden, French village, all in one walled estate. Kahn was a banker who traveled the world and tried to recreate it.
  • Afternoon: Piscine Molitor — the iconic 1929 Art Deco pool where the bikini was invented and debuted in 1946. Now a hotel and pool club. Buy a day pass for the outdoor pool. Gorgeous architecture, great bar.
  • Evening back toward the island.

Restaurants

Lunch
Piscine Molitor Pool Bar
2 avenue de la Porte Molitor, 16th · Lunch at the pool itself. Rosé, a good burger or salad, Art Deco tiles. The vibe is the point. Do it on a warm day — this is the day.
Dinner
Huîtrerie Régis
3 rue de Montfaucon, 6th · A tiny oyster bar in Saint-Germain, 12 seats, exceptional oysters from Marennes-Oléron. Champagne or Muscadet. One of Paris's most perfect small meals. Arrive early, no reservations.
Alt Dinner
Le Comptoir du Relais
9 carrefour de l'Odéon, 6th · Yves Camdeborde's beloved bistro. Dinner is prix-fixe, no reservations, line up. The food is extraordinary — proper bistronomie from the man who started the movement.

Saturday, June 7
Day Trip — Provins Medieval City
📍 Provins · 1.5 hrs from Paris Est
⛅71°F · Comfortable

Day Plan

  • Train from Gare de l'Est → Provins (~1 hr 25 min, Transilien). Leave by 9 am.
  • Provins — a UNESCO-listed medieval fortified town most Americans have never heard of. The upper town is ringed by 12th-century ramparts. The Tour César is perfectly preserved. The rose gardens (Provins = the rose town) are in full bloom in early June.
  • Explore the underground tunnels (chalk gallery network, guided tours available) and walk the full rampart circuit.
  • Return train to Paris by 6 pm.

Restaurants

Lunch
La Croix Blanche — Provins
In the upper town of Provins. Traditional French cuisine in a medieval setting. Stone walls, beams, good fixed-price menu. The local specialty is rose-flavored everything — try the rose petal jam.
Dinner
Frenchie
5 rue du Nil, 2nd · If you haven't been yet — tonight's the night. Gregory Marchand's market-driven menu changes constantly. Reserve well ahead. One of the best restaurants in Paris.
Alt Dinner
Saturne
17 rue Notre-Dame-des-Victoires, 2nd · Natural wine + Nordic-French cooking. If you missed it in Week 1, this is your second chance. The wine list alone is worth the reservation.

Sunday, June 8
Montmartre — The Real Version
📍 18th arr · Montmartre
☀️74°F · Sunny

Day Plan

  • Go to Montmartre but avoid the tourist Montmartre. Walk up via rue Lepic — the village market street. Stop at the Café des Deux Moulins for breakfast (15 rue Lepic — yes, the Amélie café).
  • Clos Montmartre — Paris's own vineyard, planted in 1933. Tiny, beautiful, between rue des Saules and rue Saint-Vincent. Almost always empty.
  • Musée de Montmartre (12 rue Cortot) — Renoir painted Le Moulin de la Galette from this garden. Utrillo's and Valadon's studios. Very intimate.
  • Evening: Jazz at Le Caveau de la Huchette (5 rue de la Huchette, 5th) — 8 pm doors, €15 entry. A 1940s jazz cave, Louis Armstrong played here. Walk there from the island in 10 minutes.

Restaurants

Lunch
Café des Deux Moulins
15 rue Lepic, 18th · Yes, the Amélie café — but it's a real neighborhood café too. Croque madame, café crème, terrace on rue Lepic. Breakfast or late lunch before walking Montmartre.
Dinner
Le Caveau de la Huchette
5 rue de la Huchette, 5th · Dinner + jazz in the cave below. The food is not the point — the atmosphere is. A 1940s jazz club, under vaulted stone arches, 10 min walk from your hotel. Go for the night.
Alt Dinner
Brasserie de l'Isle Saint-Louis
55 quai de Bourbon · Walk home from Montmartre via the Marais and end at the island brasserie. River view, cold beer, steak frites. A perfect Sunday night.

Monday, June 9
La Recyclerie · Petite Ceinture
📍 18th arr · Abandoned Railway
🌤73°F · Partly sunny

Day Plan

  • La Recyclerie (83 boulevard Ornano, 18th) — a café, urban farm, repair shop, and bar built inside the old Ornano train station on the Petite Ceinture. Chickens, a vegetable garden, beehives, a cocktail bar. Completely unique Paris space.
  • Walk a section of the Petite Ceinture — Paris's abandoned circular railway, some sections open to the public. Overgrown, wild, beautiful. Access at boulevard Ornano or Parc de la Porte-Brancion.
  • Afternoon: visit the nearby Marché aux Puces de la Porte de Vanves (Saturdays and Sundays — skip if Monday) or just explore the 14th arr neighborhood.

Restaurants

Lunch
La Recyclerie
83 bd Ornano, 18th · Lunch at the urban farm café itself. Farm-to-table, vegetables grown on-site, seasonal menu. The space is unlike anything else in Paris. Eat outside in the old station courtyard.
Dinner
Septime
80 rue de Charonne, 11th · If you weren't able to get in earlier in the trip, try for a cancellation. Worth every effort. One of the best restaurants in Europe right now.
Alt Dinner
Les Fous de l'Île
33 rue des Deux Ponts, 4th · Neighbourhood bistro on the island. Quiet Monday option, close to home, genuinely good cooking. The île flottante (floating island dessert) here is excellent.

Tuesday, June 10
Palais Royal · Covered Passages
📍 1st & 2nd arr · Right Bank
☀️75°F · Beautiful

Day Plan

  • Palais Royal gardens — the locals sit here. Arcaded colonnades, fountains, Daniel Buren's striped columns. Galleries beneath the arches — good antiquarian books, rare stamps, toy soldiers.
  • Walk the covered passages — 19th-century glass-roofed shopping arcades, frozen in time. Start at Galerie Vivienne (most beautiful, mosaics and rotunda), then Passage Jouffroy (wax museum entrance, quirky shops), then Passage des Panoramas (Paris's oldest, 1799, now stamp dealers and vintage postcards).
  • Stroll the rue du Nil food street (Frenchie zone — butcher, cheese shop, grocery, oyster bar all on one block).

Restaurants

Lunch
Le Grand Véfour
17 rue de Beaujolais · Inside the Palais Royal colonnade. One of the oldest restaurants in Paris (1784). Gorgeous Second Empire décor. Michelin-starred. A splurge lunch if you want one truly grand meal.
Dinner
Frenchie
5 rue du Nil, 2nd · If you haven't been — this is the night. Market menu, changes daily, brilliant. The bar à vins across the street (Frenchie Bar à Vins) is walk-in and excellent for a pre-dinner glass.
Alt Dinner
Café Ruc
159 rue Saint-Honoré, 1st · Smart brasserie near Palais Royal. Terrace in summer, classic French menu done very well. Good for a relaxed, beautiful last-full-week dinner.

Date Night 1
Little Red Door + Le Potager de Charlotte
Best on a Friday or Saturday · Marais → evening → back to the island
Start with dinner at Le Potager de Charlotte — one of Paris's best fully vegan restaurants, upmarket, genuinely romantic atmosphere. Beautiful plant-based bistronomy, thoughtful wine list with natural wines (many vegan-friendly). Then walk 10 minutes through the Marais to Little Red Door (60 rue Charlot, 3rd arr). Walk-in only — arrive before 8 pm or after 10:30 pm to avoid the worst of the wait. One of the top cocktail bars in the world, 9 appearances on the World's 50 Best Bars list. Seasonal menu, locally sourced ingredients, exceptional hospitality. Walk home across Pont Marie afterward.

Painting Class + Vegan Dinner + Seine Walk

Date Night 3
Sunset Picnic on the Seine + Jazz Cave
Best on a warm evening · the island to the Latin Quarter
Assemble a vegan picnic: grab pastries from VG Pâtisserie or Cloud Cakes, pick up hummus, olives, fresh bread, fruit, and a bottle of natural wine from a local épicerie. Take it to the steps on Quai d'Anjou (north side of Île Saint-Louis) or the Pont de la Tournelle and watch the sun go down over the city — in June you're looking at a 9:45 pm golden hour. Then walk five minutes to Le Caveau de la Huchette (5 rue de la Huchette, 5th) — a 1940s jazz club under vaulted stone, doors at 8 pm, €15–20 entry. Louis Armstrong played here. Dance if you want to.

Date Night 4
Montmartre at Sunset + Vegan Pastries + Night Walk
Best on a clear evening · 18th arr into the night
Head to Montmartre in the late afternoon (5–6 pm). Walk up via rue Lepic, stop at Boneshaker Donuts (77 rue d'Aboukir, 2nd — or find the nearest location) for vegan donuts to eat while walking. Date Night 6
42 Degrés Raw Vegan Dinner + Palais Royal Night Stroll
The adventurous one · first raw vegan bistronomy restaurant in France
Take Megi to 42 Degrés — the first raw organic vegan bistronomy restaurant in France. Nothing served above 42°C to preserve nutrients. This sounds clinical but the food is genuinely beautiful and creative — Megi will love it. After dinner, walk to the Palais Royal gardens at night (they stay open late in summer) — the colonnades lit up, Daniel Buren's striped columns, the quiet fountains. One of the most romantic spots in the city and almost nobody is there after 9 pm. Pick up a vegan treat from Aujourd'hui Demain (concept store + vegan bakery, 10 rue de la Chaise, 7th) earlier in the day to bring for the walk.

📌 A few notes on vegan Paris Paris's vegan scene has exploded — HappyCow lists 90+ dedicated vegan restaurants. Almost every good bistronomie restaurant now has strong plant-based options even if they're not exclusively vegan. When in doubt, call ahead and ask — French chefs are more accommodating than their reputation suggests, especially in the better restaurants. The island itself has limited vegan-specific options, so plan dinner off-island most nights and enjoy the walk back.

Essential Tips

🚇 Getting Around

Buy a Paris Visite card or use the Navigo Découverte weekly pass (€26, buy at any metro station with a photo). Covers all zones including RER to airports. Taxis/Uber for late nights and Orly.

📱 Apps to Download

Bonjour RATP (metro maps + times), Google Maps offline (download Paris map), La Fourchette/TheFork (restaurant reservations), XE Currency, and Duolingo for a few French basics (locals genuinely appreciate the effort).

💳 Money

Most places accept Visa/Mastercard. Always carry €50–100 cash — several of the best restaurants (Ma Cuisine, Le Baron Rouge) are cash-only. Withdraw from ATMs inside bank branches, not standalone machines.

🍷 Wine Buying in Burgundy

You can bring 5 litres of wine back to the US duty-free as a returning resident. Pack bubble wrap and check a dedicated wine bag. The Époisses cheese: wrap in multiple layers, keep cool, declare it (soft ripened cheese is fine to bring home).

🏨 Hotel Booking — Urgent

Hôtel du Jeu de Paume is the top pick for June 3–12. Book direct at jeudepaumehotel.com — direct rates are typically better than OTAs. Also consider Hôtel L de Lutèce (paris-hotel-lutece.com) as backup. Both on Île Saint-Louis.

🍽 Reservations to Make Now

Book Septime, Saturne, Frenchie, Le Sergent Recruteur, and Le Benaton (Beaune) immediately — all book weeks out. Le Baron Rouge, Chez Janou, and Brasserie de l'Isle Saint-Louis are walk-in.
